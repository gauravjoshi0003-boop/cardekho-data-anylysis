# 🚗 CarDekho - Used Car Market Data Analysis & Insights (SQL)

## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing the used car market using the CarDekho dataset. The objective is to extract meaningful business insights regarding pricing strategies, brand positioning, customer preferences (fuel and owner types), and performance factors. 

By analyzing these metrics, data-driven recommendations can be provided to car marketplaces and dealerships to optimize their inventory management, estimate accurate car valuations, and understand market demand.

## 🛠️ Tech Stack & Tools Used
* **Database Management System:** MySQL / MySQL Workbench
* **Language:** Structured Query Language (SQL)
* **Concepts Applied:** Data Filtering, Aggregations, Grouping, Sorting, and Window Functions.

##  Key Business Questions Solved (SQL Queries)

Here are the critical business insights generated from the dataset using SQL:

1. Top 5 Most Expensive Brands (Premium Segment Analysis)
Helps dealers understand which brands command the highest average resale value and premium margins in the market.


2. Fuel Type vs. Mileage & Market Demand (Cost-Benefit Analysis)
Analyzes how different fuel types affect the average pricing and efficiency (mileage) of vehicles, and evaluates their overall availability in the market.

3. High Power, Low Price Vehicles (Value-for-Money Recommendations)
Identifies top performance-oriented cars (high max power) available within a budget segment (under 5 Lakhs) for budget-conscious buyers.

4. Owner Type Analysis (Market Depreciation Study)
Investigates how the number of previous owners impacts the average resale value of vehicles.

Executive Summary & Key Insights
Market Dominance: High-volume brands like Maruti and Hyundai dominate market availability, making them the safest bet for high-turnover inventory.

Fuel Trends: Petrol and Diesel vehicles hold the maximum market share. While Petrol cars offer a lower initial purchase cost, Diesel cars maintain a strong presence due to better mileage efficiency over long distances.

Depreciation Impact: Vehicle valuation drops significantly with each successive ownership change. First-owner cars retain the highest market value, whereas a sharp decline is seen from second to third-party owners.
